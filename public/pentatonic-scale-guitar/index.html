The five-note formula

Remove two notes. Keep the character.

The minor pentatonic uses scale degrees 1, ♭3, 4, 5, and ♭7. In A, those notes are A, C, D, E, and G. The missing second and sixth reduce half-step clashes, which makes the scale approachable for improvisation.

Common questions

Pentatonic scale FAQ

Which pentatonic box should I learn first?

Start with Box 1, then learn the neighboring Box 2 and Box 5. Connecting nearby positions is more useful than memorizing five disconnected diagrams.

What notes are in A minor pentatonic?

A, C, D, E, and G—the root, minor third, fourth, fifth, and minor seventh.

Can I use minor pentatonic over major chords?

Often, especially in blues and rock, but the sound depends on the harmony. Listen to how the minor third rubs against a major third in the chord and resolve it intentionally.
